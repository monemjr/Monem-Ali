# Philosophy

I became a director because filmmaking became the only language capable of expressing what words couldn't.

Since I was a child, I noticed details that many people overlooked. I didn't know that one day those observations would become the foundation of my work.

I don't search for extraordinary stories.

I search for ordinary people, because every human being carries a story worth telling.

I believe the eyes reveal emotions before words do.

I want my films to make people feel seen.

If someone watches my work and quietly says,

"That feels like me."

then the film has already succeeded.

As long as there are people to understand, stories to discover, and emotions left unspoken, I will continue making films.
